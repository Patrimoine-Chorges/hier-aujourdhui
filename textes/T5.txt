Autrefois appelée Place du Marché, la place qui doit son nom à François de Bonne de Lesdiguières dernier connétable de France, est à l’époque moderne, le cœur du village. En son centre, la fontaine du XVIe siècle rappelle le rôle essentiel de l’eau dans la vie quotidienne : on venait y remplir les seaux, faire boire les animaux, laver le linge, mais aussi, discuter et échanger les nouvelles. Bien plus qu’un simple point d’eau, la fontaine était un véritable lieu de vie et de rencontre. 

Formerly known as Place du Marché, the square takes its present name from François de Bonne, Duke of Lesdiguières and the last Constable of France. During the early modern period, it formed the heart of the village. At its centre, the 16th-century fountain recalls the essential role of water in everyday life: people came to fill their buckets, water their animals and wash their clothes, but also to talk and exchange news. Far more than a simple water source, the fountain was a lively gathering place at the centre of community life.
